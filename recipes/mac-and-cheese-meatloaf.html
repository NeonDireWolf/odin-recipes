Mach and Cheese Meatloaf

Description

A hearty twist on classic meatloaf, stuffed and topped with creamy macaroni and cheese for the ultimate comfort food mashup.

Ingredients

Meatloaf

  • 1/4 cup butter
  • 1/4 cup minced onion
  • 2 cloves garlic, grated
  • 2 lbs lean ground beef
  • 30 buttery round crackers, crushed
  • 1 large egg, lightly beaten
  • 3 1/2 tablespoons sour cream
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 (15 ounce) can tomato sauce
  • 1 tablespoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 cup ketchup
  • 1 cup barbecue sauce

Mac & Cheese

  • 3 cups macaroni noodles
  • 4 tablespoons butter
  • 1/4 cup all purpose flour
  • 4 cups milk
  • 4 cups shredded mozzarella and cheddar cheese blend
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoons ground black pepper

Topping

  • 1/4 cup melted butter
  • 3/4 cup panko breadcrumbs

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 375 degrees F
  2. For meatloaf, melt butter in a skillet over medium heat, and cook the onion and garlic until the onion is translucent, about 5 minutes.
  3. Combine onion mixture, beef, crushed crackers, egg, sour cream, Worcestershire sauce, 1/2 can tomato sauce, onion powder, salt, and pepper in a large bowl. Mix until well combined. Transfer mixture to a 9x13 baking dish and spread into an even layer.
  4. Combine remaining tomato sauce, ketchup, and barbecue sauce in a small bowl. Spread sauce evenly over meat mixture.
  5. Bake in the preheated oven for 20 minutes.
  6. Meanwhile, for mac & cheese, bring a large pot of salted water to a boil; cook macaroni until al dente, 8 to 10 minutes. Drain, and return to the pot.
  7. Melt 1/4 cup butter in a saucepan over medium heat. Whisk in flour. Slowly pour in milk while continuing to whisk. Once milk is heated through add cheese, 1 cup at a time, continuing to whisk, until cheese has melted and sauce is smooth. Season with salt and pepper
  8. Pour cheese sauce over noodles and stir until noodles are well coated.
  9. For topping, stir melted butter and panko together in a small bowl.
  10. Once meatloaf comes out of the oven, pour macaroni over meatloaf in an even layer. Top evenly with panko mixture.
  11. Return to the oven; bake until bubbly and golden brown, 10 to 15 minutes. Remove the dish from the oven and allow to cool for 5 to 10 minutes before serving.
